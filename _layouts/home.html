Research

As a PhD student in Nancy Chen's lab, I’m interested in using genomics, computational biology, and population genetics to better understand how inbreeding impacts the fitness of small, threatened populations. Specifically, I’m investigating the underlying genetic architecture of inbreeding depression: how does elevated inbreeding manifest in the genome, and what regions of the genome contribute to fitness, and ultimately, population decline? During my undergraduate at UC Berkeley, I used museum specimens to determine how Anna’s Hummingbirds have responded to human-induced environmental change.
